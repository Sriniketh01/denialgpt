CMS NCD 220.2 — Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)
Coverage Indications and Medical Necessity

MRI is covered when ordered by a treating physician and when
the service is reasonable and necessary for the diagnosis or
treatment of illness or injury.

For musculoskeletal MRI of the extremities (including knee),
coverage requires:

1. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION: Many Medicare Advantage and commercial
payers including Aetna require prior authorization for
outpatient MRI of the extremities before the service is
rendered. Claims submitted without prior authorization will
be denied.

2. CONSERVATIVE THERAPY REQUIREMENT: MRI of the knee is
considered medically necessary when the patient has
persistent joint pain that is unresponsive to conservative
treatment for a minimum of 3-6 weeks. Conservative therapy
includes: physical therapy (PT), non-steroidal
anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), rest, ice, compression,
and elevation. Documentation of failed conservative therapy
must be present in the medical record.

3. CLINICAL INDICATIONS FOR KNEE MRI (CPT 73721):
- Suspected meniscal tear with mechanical symptoms (locking,
  catching, giving way) after failed conservative therapy
- Suspected ligamentous injury (ACL, PCL, MCL, LCL) not
  resolved with conservative management
- Osteoarthritis (M17.11) with persistent pain unresponsive
  to 3+ months of conservative therapy when surgical
  planning is being considered
- Evaluation of cartilage damage not visible on X-ray
- Osteonecrosis or avascular necrosis of the knee

4. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Physician order with clinical indication
- Evidence of failed conservative therapy (PT notes, NSAID
  prescription history, duration of treatment)
- Prior authorization confirmation number from payer
- Relevant prior imaging results (X-ray must precede MRI
  in most cases)

5. DENIAL RISK FACTORS for CPT 73721 + M17.11 (Aetna):
- Missing prior authorization: claim will be denied
  (CARC 50 or CARC 15)
- Insufficient conservative therapy documentation: less
  than 3-6 weeks documented therapy
- No clinical justification linking MRI to treatment
  decision or surgical planning
- X-ray not performed prior to MRI order

Sources: CMS NCD 220.2, Aetna Clinical Policy Bulletin
No. 0171 (MRI of the Extremities)
